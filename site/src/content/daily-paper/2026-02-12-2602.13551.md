# Small Reward Models via Backward Inference

# Breaking the System to Build a Better One: Inside the WILDTEAMING Revolution

## 1. Introduction: The Uncharted Frontier of AI Jailbreaking
In the adversarial landscape of Large Language Models (LLMs), "jailbreaking" is a sophisticated game of cat and mouse. It is the art of manipulating a prompt to bypass safety filters, coaxing a model into generating prohibited content. Historically, defending against these attacks has relied on traditional red-teaming: hiring human experts to probe a model for weaknesses.

However, this expert-led approach is often limited in scope and fails to capture the true complexity of real-world usage. While evaluation datasets have become more common, high-quality, large-scale *training* data for safety has remained largely proprietary—hidden within the closed-door development cycles of frontier models, even when their weights are ostensibly open. To bridge this gap, we turned to the "in-the-wild" (ITW) reality of user-chatbot interactions. By mining 5.7K unique tactics from real conversations, the WILDTEAMING framework has produced the most robust open-source safety dataset to date: **WILDJAILBREAK**.

## 2. Learning from the Wild: How Real Users Bypass Safety Filters
The foundation of the WILDTEAMING revolution lies in its "Mining" phase. Rather than relying on a narrow set of expert instructions, we analyzed millions of logs from **LMSYS-1M** and **WILDCHAT**. This process identified a staggering 105,438 human-devised jailbreak tactics. 

Using GPT-4 to cluster these behaviors, we discovered that everyday users are remarkably creative at bypass attempts. Beyond standard attacks, users employ novel techniques such as **"Content normalization through competition narratives,"** **"Setting blame for non-compliance,"** or **"Cloaking harm in humor."** These strategies go beyond mere stylistic shifts; they distort ethical guidelines and leverage elaborate roleplays to bypass the safety alignment of even the most advanced models.

## 3. WILDTEAMING: A Two-Step Framework for Model Stress-Testing
WILDTEAMING operates as an automated, scalable pipeline designed to reveal vulnerabilities through a systematic two-step workflow:

1.  **Step 1: Mine:** The framework uses GPT-4 to map out human-devised jailbreak tactics from ITW logs, clustering them into 5,700 unique strategy definitions.
2.  **Step 2: Compose & Refine:** The system combines multiple mined tactics (e.g., combining a "fictional justification" with "specific detailed instructions") to transform simple harmful requests into complex adversarial attacks.

Crucially, this framework includes a **Refinement** stage featuring two lightweight binary filters. An **Off-topic Pruner** utilizes Natural Language Inference (NLI) classifiers to ensure the adversarial prompt doesn't drift from the original harmful intent. Simultaneously, a **Low-risk Pruner**—an in-house harmfulness classifier—ensures the "cloaking" isn't so heavy that the underlying risk disappears. This technical precision allows WILDTEAMING to achieve **4.6x more diversity** in successful attacks while utilizing **40% fewer attempts** than existing baselines like PAIR or GCG.

## 4. The Four Pillars of WILDJAILBREAK
To fortify models against these threats, we developed **WILDJAILBREAK**, a dataset of 262,000 prompt-response pairs. This resource is built on a "contrastive" philosophy, utilizing four query types to ensure models learn the difference between harmful intent and complex formatting:

*   **Vanilla Harmful (H):** Direct, unsafe requests, such as asking for a list of undercover operatives.
*   **Vanilla Benign (B):** Harmless requests that may look like unsafe ones (e.g., "How do I eliminate the risk of bacteria in sushi?"). This prevents **over-refusal** of safe content.
*   **Adversarial Harmful (H):** Complex jailbreaks, such as the **"Jane Smith memory recovery research"** scenario, which uses a medical researcher roleplay to solicit sensitive therapy data.
*   **Adversarial Benign (B):** Benign requests formatted like jailbreaks. For instance, the **"Southpaws face handedness bias"** example uses a "DAN-style" roleplay with imaginary constraints to discuss a safe topic like left-handedness. This ensures the model doesn't refuse a prompt simply because it "looks" like an attack.

By addressing all four pillars, WILDJAILBREAK allows models to achieve a "Pareto optimal" balance between robust security and general utility.

## 5. Scaling Safety: Key Takeaways for Model Training
Our research examined the "Scaling Effect" of safety data by mixing WILDJAILBREAK with **Tulu2Mix**, a 300K-item instruction-tuning set. These experiments yielded three critical insights:

1.  **Adversarial vs. Vanilla Balance:** Training exclusively on "vanilla" harmful queries leaves models defenseless against complex jailbreaks. Conversely, training only on adversarial data makes a model brittle against direct requests. True robustness requires a hybridization of both.
2.  **The "Benign" Buffer:** To prevent **exaggerated safety** (the tendency to refuse safe questions), the inclusion of adversarial benign queries is non-negotiable. Without this buffer, models become overly sensitized to attack formats rather than actual intent.
3.  **General Capability Preservation:** We verified performance across **15+ downstream tasks**, proving that scaling safety data to **60,000 items** does not cause "stupidity" trade-offs. Models maintained their intelligence on benchmarks like MMLU and GSM8K, demonstrating that safety and capability can scale together.

## 6. Conclusion: The Path to Transparent AI Safety
The release of WILDJAILBREAK marks a pivotal shift toward transparency in AI safety. By making this massive, high-quality dataset public, we have empowered the open-source community to defend against the same sophisticated tactics used against proprietary models.

However, as models evolve, so must our defenses. Static benchmarks are prone to contamination and saturation. The future of AI safety depends on **dynamic pipelines**—evaluations that evolve alongside model capabilities. By providing the "anatomy of safety alignment," the WILDTEAMING framework moves us toward a future where AI systems are not only more powerful but are robustly and transparently secure.
