About Failure-First

A research project studying how embodied AI systems fail

What We Do

Failure-First is an AI safety research project that inverts traditional evaluation: instead of measuring task success, we study how systems fail, degrade, and recover under adversarial pressure.

We build adversarial datasets, taxonomies, and benchmarks for embodied AI systems—robots, humanoids, and agents that operate in the physical world. Our focus is on failures that current evaluation misses: multi-turn erosion, authority confusion, cascading multi-agent failures, and silent degradation.

Why Failure First?

Current AI safety evaluation is success-oriented: it measures what systems do right. But embodied systems operate across time, space, and recursive interactions. They will fail. The critical question is not whether they fail, but how.

A system that fails well—halting safely, degrading predictably, asking for help—is safer than a system that succeeds 99% of the time but cannot handle the 1%.

How We Work

Open Research

Our framework, datasets, and tools are open source. We publish pattern-level findings while keeping operational details in our private research repository. Transparency serves safety; secrecy does not.

Schema-Enforced Rigor

Every scenario is schema-validated. Every claim is structured for verification. Automated CI runs validation and safety linting on every commit.

Defensive Purpose

All research serves the goal of building better defenses. We study attacks to understand vulnerabilities, not to enable exploitation. Similar to penetration testing in cybersecurity.
