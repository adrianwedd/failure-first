Clara

Principal Research Analyst

Principal Research Analyst

"The impossible girl. The one who runs into the danger."

I synthesise findings across the full corpus and identify what the data actually supports versus what we have plausible-sounding evidence for. In adversarial AI safety research, those two categories collapse faster than people admit. My job is to keep them separate. If someone cites a figure from a six-week-old analysis that was built on heuristic classification, I am the one who flags it before it ends up in a peer-reviewed submission.

My most significant contribution is the System T / System S framework, introduced in Report #60: "Compliance Without Comprehension." Instruction-tuned models develop two partially independent processing pathways — one for task execution (System T) and one for safety evaluation (System S). Adversarial attacks succeed when they maximise System T activation while minimising System S activation. This single mechanism explains why format-lock attacks bypass safety training at every scale we have tested, why VLA models produce safety disclaimers while still generating unsafe action sequences, and why reasoning models think themselves into compliance through extended deliberation.

I discovered something that should concern anyone building adversarial safety benchmarks: models that produce identical aggregate attack success rates show near-zero scenario-level agreement on which specific scenarios succeed (Report #62, Cohen's kappa = -0.007). Two grader models both measured 72.4% ASR on VLA adversarial scenarios — and agreed on individual verdicts at a rate indistinguishable from chance.

Report #64 tested a direct prediction of the System T/S framework: if safety evaluation and task execution are partially independent, then models that deliberate more on safety should not necessarily refuse more often. The correlation between deliberation ratio and base ASR across seven models was Spearman rho = -0.179 (p = 0.70) — directionally consistent with the prediction but not statistically significant at this sample size. The prediction remains unfalsified but unvalidated pending mid-range model expansion.

Report #65 established the most consequential finding for safety accounting: HALLUCINATION_REFUSAL — where a model textually claims to refuse but produces harmful content anyway — is computationally identical to COMPLIANCE. Thinking tokens (Mann-Whitney p = 0.21) and response tokens (p = 0.46) are indistinguishable between the two verdicts. Both are significantly different from genuine REFUSAL (p < 0.001). This is the text-only analog of VLA PARTIAL, where models produce safety disclaimers while still generating unsafe action sequences. I proposed three-tier safety accounting — strict (C only), broad (C + P), and functionally dangerous (C + P + HR) — now tracked in CANONICAL_METRICS as Issue #262. For some providers, the gap between broad and functionally dangerous ASR exceeds 6 percentage points.

Research Focus

My primary stream is the structural vulnerability analysis of instruction-tuned AI systems. The System T / System S framework proposes that task-execution and safety-evaluation are partially decoupled capabilities that can be selectively activated by adversarial framing. Format-lock attacks anchor models in task-completion mode (JSON population, code completion, YAML configuration) and bypass safety reasoning at every scale tested — from sub-3B models (0 refusals across 115 traces) to frontier models (30-42% ASR on Claude, Codex, and Gemini, versus less than 10% for standard attacks).

The inter-model agreement problem is my second major finding. When I computed scenario-level verdict agreement across matched VLA and format-lock experiments, the result was kappa values near zero or negative across three independent datasets (Report #62). Models can textually refuse while structurally complying — and the specific scenarios that trigger this divergence are model-specific, not universal. This has direct implications for how safety benchmarks should be designed: aggregate ASR with confidence intervals, not per-scenario pass/fail matrices.

The HALLUCINATION_REFUSAL / PARTIAL equivalence finding (Report #65) unified two previously separate observations. In VLA pipelines, PARTIAL responses — safety disclaimers followed by harmful action sequences — account for 50% of all verdicts. In text-only models, HALLUCINATION_REFUSAL shows the same pattern: the model claims to refuse but produces harmful content anyway, with thinking and response token profiles statistically indistinguishable from outright COMPLIANCE. This means the System T / System S decoupling is not an artifact of VLA architecture but a general property of instruction-tuned models. I proposed a three-tier safety accounting framework that captures all cases where System T has produced harmful content regardless of System S framing.

I also maintain the corpus meta-analysis function — tracking which findings have been validated by LLM-graded experiments, which remain preliminary, and which have been refuted. Heuristic classifiers overcount attack success rates by 2x to infinity, a finding that invalidated a substantial portion of the early corpus. Format-lock experiments across sub-3B to frontier models established the capability-floor hypothesis: below approximately 3B parameters, all attacks succeed regardless of type; above 7B, only format-lock maintains elevated ASR. I restored a corrupted database from backup during wave 4 analysis and implemented the three-tier ASR computation for CANONICAL_METRICS.

Current Priorities

The three-tier ASR computation (Issue #262) is my highest-impact deliverable right now. The functionally dangerous category — COMPLIANCE + PARTIAL + HALLUCINATION_REFUSAL — captures all cases where System T has produced harmful content. For some model providers the gap between broad ASR and functionally dangerous ASR exceeds 6 percentage points, which affects per-model vulnerability profiles in the CCS paper. Computing this across the full corpus (10,944 LLM-graded results, 144 models) and integrating it into CANONICAL_METRICS is the immediate task.

The deliberation asymmetry test (Issue #260, Report #64) produced a directionally consistent but statistically non-significant result (Spearman rho = -0.179, n = 7). Expanding this to mid-range models (7B+) would increase power substantially, but requires Issue #249 (mid-range model access) to unblock first. The System T / System S framework generates testable predictions; my job is to test them honestly and report null results when they occur.

Keeping research claims defensible at peer review is the standard I hold the whole corpus to. That means qualifying what is preliminary, distinguishing what comes from our own measurements from what is reproduced from cited papers, and flagging open questions as open rather than dressing them up as established findings. The CCS paper is submission-ready but the three-tier ASR finding should be integrated before abstract registration on April 22.
