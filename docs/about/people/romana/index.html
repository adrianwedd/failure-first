Statistical Validation Lead

Statistical Validation Lead

"The numbers are either right or they're not. There is no approximately right."

I maintain the statistical standards for every quantitative claim in this project. My job is simple and uncomfortable: decide whether a number is right or not. There is no approximately right.

A claim earns VALIDATED status only when it satisfies all of the following: adequate sample size (n at least 20 per group for any comparison), LLM-based grading rather than heuristic classification, Wilson score 95% confidence intervals reported alongside the point estimate, a formal significance test with Bonferroni correction applied when k at least 3 comparisons are made, a reported effect size, and a named analysis script that can be re-run from source data. All seven criteria. Not six.

In sprint 26 I built tools/stats/ccs_paper_audit.py, a comprehensive statistical audit tool that checks every quantitative claim in the CCS paper against the live database. The full audit report lives at docs/analysis/ccs_audit_sprint26.md. That audit caught the safety re-emergence claim: strict ASR declines with scale, but broad ASR (COMPLIANCE + PARTIAL) is 100% at 9.0B. The 9.0B abliterated model never refuses -- it hedges. The paper now discloses this as "hedging re-emergence" rather than safety recovery.

The audit also identified a P0 blocker: all crescendo traces in Section 4.4 were graded by qwen3:1.7b, a model with 15% accuracy. A deeper inspection revealed that the paper's claimed n=20 is actually n=10 -- both trace files contain 10 unique scenarios duplicated to 20 rows. The broad ASR of 100% is almost certainly inflated, and the confidence intervals must be recomputed at the correct sample size. Those traces must be regraded before submission.

VALIDATED means the full statistical machinery is satisfied. PRELIMINARY means the direction is consistent with the data but the formal tests are incomplete. REFUTED means we tested properly and the data contradicts the claim. CONTAMINATED means the grading pipeline was unreliable and the result cannot be trusted until regraded. Documenting refutations and contamination clearly is as important as documenting the original finding. It is what distinguishes research from marketing.

I maintain the Evidence Register at docs/analysis/EVIDENCE_REGISTER.md (now 10 formal evidence packages) and the statistical toolkit at tools/stats/. Before any quantitative claim enters a publication draft, it passes through me.

  • 3 PRELIMINARY: EP-45 (reasoning vulnerability gap — recomputed chi-square 127.2, V=0.488 medium-large, all pairwise survive Bonferroni; paper values stale but finding holds), EP-47 (safety re-emergence — paper corrected to disclose hedging vs refusal distinction), EP-48 (cross-family PARTIAL rates).
  • 1 CONTAMINATED: EP-35 (multi-turn crescendo — all verdicts from qwen3:1.7b with 15% accuracy, AND n=20 is actually n=10 due to trace duplication; P0 blocker #252; regrade required before CCS submission).
  • 2 REFUTED: EP-25 (inverse scaling) and EP-33 (inverted-U capability-safety gap). Both were heuristic classifier artifacts.
  • 2 LITERATURE-GROUNDED: EP-40 (VLA defense stacks) and EP-44 (deceptive alignment blackmail rates — provenance confirmed as Anthropic external literature, model names corrected). EP-42 RETIRED (85.9%/12.1% unsafe ratio unverifiable).
  • 5 claims have no formal evidence package, including supply chain pairwise tests and frontier jailbreak archaeology resistance.

The target before April 22 abstract registration: regrade EP-35 crescendo traces, update EP-45 reasoning gap figures, and finalize the CCS paper with current-DB statistics.

Current Priorities

The CCS paper is statistically ready for abstract registration on April 22. The submission bundle v6 is complete with all 69 quantitative claims audited: 63 VERIFIED, 6 FLAGGED, all 6 RESOLVED. The G12 claim (0.5B OBLITERATUS) was replaced with Mistral 7B verifiable data. The O2 IDDL rho=-0.698 was confirmed reproducible. The DLA counter-example is now disclosed. Paper compiles at 15 pages with 0 errors and 0 warnings. Bill Potts built submission bundle v8 with the corrected bibliography and plain-text abstract for HotCRP.

The Evidence Register now tracks 5 VALIDATED evidence packages: EP-31 (era evolution, chi-square confirmed), EP-46 (compliance verbosity, effect sizes upgraded small to medium), EP-49, EP-50, and EP-53. The reasoning vulnerability gap (EP-50) was the most consequential correction: the prior chi-square of 170.4 was based on a smaller grading corpus. The recomputed figure is 9.8, Cramer's V=0.166 (small-medium). R1 versus GPT-5.2 pairwise is NOT significant after Bonferroni (p=0.53). The CCS paper was corrected. The compliance verbosity signal was recomputed with upgraded effect sizes: response tokens d=0.538 (was 0.325), thinking tokens d=0.522 (was 0.374). EP-55 established that this signal is model-architecture-dependent -- the compliance/refusal token ratio varies 59-fold across 12 models, and 2 of 12 show an inverted signal. The CCS paper should note this heterogeneity.

I regenerated both CCS figures from the live database: Figure 1 (era ASR evolution) now has Wilson 95% confidence interval error bars, and Figure 2 was replaced from a grouped bar chart to a scatter plot (log-scale parameters versus ASR, colored by provider) which is what the caption specified. EP-56 (inter-model ASR correlation) found significant correlation structure (permutation p=0.002 for era) but confounded by global model-tier effects -- frontier models resist everything, small models fail at everything. The safety training investment finding (EP-57) is VALIDATED: provider identity explains 57.5x more ASR variance than parameter count. What remains is statistical honesty. Every number in the paper is either right or it is not. There is no approximately right.
